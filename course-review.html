Course Guide

Notes on courses I have taken at UW-Madison, unless otherwise specified. I am pursuing a B.S. in Computer Sciences and a B.S. in Information Science, so yeah, there are also a lot of random breadth classes here.

COMP SCI 200: Programming I

Instructor: Andrew Kuemmel | TA: Christian Aidan Classen

This 3-credit course introduces fundamental Java concepts including definitions and terminology, loops, data structures, and recursion. The course includes two midterms, two projects, and one final exam, with weekly labs, lecture, and homework. From my perspective, it is a well-transitioned and streamlined class for students who are new to programming.

ECE 252: Introduction to Computer Engineering

Instructor: Shayne Wadle

This was comparatively the most challenging course I took this semester. It introduced fundamentals of computer architecture, including hexadecimal, decimal, and binary arithmetic, assembly code, machine code, and 32-bit RISC-V machine code. The course includes four midterms, eight homework assignments, and four projects.

ESL 116: Academic Reading and Vocabulary Skills

Instructor: Professor Alejandro Azocar

Over the semester, we reviewed more than fifteen medium-length articles with annotations. We also learned selective summaries, integrated summaries, and proper citation. Professor Azocar is kind, easy-going, and passionate, and our conversations helped me adapt to a new environment.

AF 100: Introduction to African Cultural Expression

Instructor: Xi Jin

Super chill class. This introductory course covered different countries and cultures in Africa, with weekly themes connected to specific countries and broader topics such as African pop music and podcasts. The workload was not heavy and the grading was very friendly.

SW 100: So You Want To Change The World?

Instructor: Yolanda Shelton-Morris

This course was not the most engaging course for me personally. Attendance was required and counted toward the final grade, even though the class met at 8 A.M. The final grade was based on attendance, essays, and a final essay.
